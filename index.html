Canada's First Subway

Date: September 8, 1949

Photographer: Canada Pictures Limited

Description: The photo shows the groundbreaking ceremony happening on Yonge Street and Wellington Street. The man in black is Ray Lawson, Lieutenant Governor of Ontario, he is pushing a switch to drive the first steel pile.

Date: May 16, 1951

Photographer: Canada Pictures Limited

Description: The construction team was digging a tunnel and people were interested in this new construction and gathered around the fence to watch them work. But the construction seemed to be taking up the road and causing traffic congestion.

Date: December 3, 1949

Photographer: Canada Pictures Limited

Description: To allow traffic to run normally, the construction team laid a layer of thick wooden boards above the open-air tunnel. This ensured that traffic could continue as usual during construction. These wooden boards are durable enough, that even a tram can be seen behind the street.

Date: March 17, 1952

Photographer: Canada Pictures Limited

Description: Three workers are currently pouring the floor of the station. The station has been capped and the surrounding concrete walls have been completed, but it has not yet been decorated. This means that the entire station is close to completion.

Date: June 12, 1952

Photographer: Canada Pictures Limited

Description: This is the inside of a subway car. There is a row of windows on both sides of the car, and above the windows are maps and advertisements. There are two rows of lights on the ceiling, so you can read inside the car. The seats are leather and very comfortable to sit on. It looks similar to a bus.
